Rules Poker

The game can be played by 2 to 10 people. Each player in Texas Hold'em is dealt two cards.

After the distribution, the betting begins, which takes place over several rounds.

The two players to the left of the dealer make mandatory bets, which are charged before the start of bidding. This is done in order to encourage players to play actively. These mandatory bets are called blinds. These are the rules of poker.

If you are a beginner, then know that during the hand it is more profitable to have a late position in order to track the moves of your opponents. These are not poker rules, but rather a kind of tactical move. After the players have posted the blinds, the first stage of betting begins.

The rules of the game of poker imply the presence of certain actions that the player performs in the process of trading:

  • Put, bet (eng. bet) - place a bet
  • Answer, call (eng. call) - bet the same amount as the opponent put - call
  • Raise, raise (English raise) - increase the bet - bet more than rivals
  • Discard cards, fold (eng. fold) - refuse to further participate in the game and discard cards
  • Skip, check (English check) - in situations where the bet has already been made or bets have not been made by opponents - do not add anything to the pot, leave "as is"
  • The betting round ends when all players have made equal bets or folded.

After the first round of betting, if more than one person remains in the hand, then, according to the rules of poker, three common open cards are placed on the table, which are called the flop. Community cards must be used to make combinations.

Rules Craps

The game begins with a roll of six-sided dice. They are called dice. If the marking of the cubes is correct, then the faces of the dice should total seven: six opposite one, five opposite two, four opposite three. Incorrect markup can be used in fraud.

Craps is played with a number of dice from one to five pieces (depending on the style of play) at a large table, with specific markings applied and with a considerable number of places for bets.

The game "Craps" can be divided into two main stages: Come Out Roll (the first throw of the shooter) and Point Roll (throw after the establishment of the Point number). The shooter rolls the dice, and the amount of points rolled determines the further course of the game.

If a 7 or 11 rolls, the shooter wins immediately and gets to roll the dice again. If a 2, 3, or 12 rolls, the shooter immediately loses and passes the dice to another player. If a 4, 5, 6, 8, or 9 is rolled, that number is called a Point and the game moves on to the next stage, the Point Roll. Here the shooter will roll the dice until the Point or 7 set by him falls out. In the first case, he will win, in the second he will lose.

Rules Blackjack

Simple, dynamic and addictive game. The player makes bets on one or more boxes indicated on the table. After the dealer's words "No more bets" and the gesture to close the bets with the hand, the players cannot touch and change their bets.

First, the player receives two face-up cards, the sum of the points of which allows him to decide whether he needs additional cards or not. The goal of the game is to score 21 points or close to it. If a player scores more than 21 points, then his bet loses. If the sum of points on the dealer's cards is greater than 21, then all bets remaining in the game win.

Players who score more points than the dealer win, their bets are paid 1:1. Players with less points than the dealer lose. If the sum of the player's points is equal to the sum of the dealer's points, then a "draw" or Stay is declared: the player's bet does not win or lose.

The dealer draws cards last, while he must take a card if he has 16 points or less, and stop if the total points are 17 or more. Aces are counted as 1 or 11, "pictures" (jacks, queens and kings) - all 10 points each, the rest of the cards correspond to their face value.

"BLACK JACK"

This is a combination on the first two cards from the hand, giving a total of 21 points (ace and ten or ace and “picture”). In this case, the player's bets are paid 3:2 if the dealer does not have the same combination. Blackjack wins against any other combination of cards, including combinations with a total of 21 points.

SURRENDER

After the distribution of the first two cards to all the playing boxes, the player can refuse to continue the game on any number of his boxes, losing half of the corresponding bets. This can be done before the third card hits the box they want to SURRENDER to. The player cannot SURRENDER if the dealer's first card is an ace.

SPLIT

If the player's first two cards are of the same value, that is, they have the same value in terms of points, then they can be divided. In this case, the player makes an additional bet on this box, equal to the initial one, and collects cards for each card separately. All pairs of identical cards can be split no more than three times. Aces are an exception. Splitting aces is allowed twice, and when splitting, the player receives only one card for each of the aces. Ace and ten (picture) combination obtained after the split is considered as 21 and is not a Blackjack combination.

DOUBLE

After the player has received the first two cards, regardless of the number of points, he can double his bet, while receiving only one card. Doubling is allowed after splitting the cards. If the player has 9, 10 and 11 points, then the dealer will always offer to double the player's bet.

INSURANCE (INSURANCE)

If the dealer's first card is an ace, then the player can make an additional bet, but not more than half of the initial bets and not less than half of the table minimum, placing it on the field marked Insurance (insurance). This bet wins if the dealer has Blackjack and pays 2:1, loses in all other cases.

Players can also insure their box against Blackjack at the dealer when the dealer's first card is 10. In this case, the dealer does not announce insurance.

EVEN MONEY (EQUAL MONEY)

If the player has Blackjack, and the dealer has the first card an ace, then before the start of a set of cards for the first playing box, the player can receive equal money, i.e. payment for the box, equal to his bet 1:1. If the player refuses equal money, and the dealer has a Blackjack, then the player's bet is not paid, i.e. it does not win and does not lose.

NOTES

Up to three players can play on one box. The bet of each of them must not be less than the minimum of the table, and the total bet on the box must not exceed the maximum of the table.

The player himself controls the number of points on his boxes and checks them for compliance with the value announced by the dealer. In case of incorrect distribution of cards or other error of the dealer, no payments are made to the players.

Pai Gow poker rules

Rules Keno

There are three ways to play in KENO. You can play in one, two or three ways at the same time. The price of the ticket varies depending on the number of ways chosen.

The first way: "Choose a combination"
There are 80 numbers in the KENO ticket. You can choose from 1 to 10 numbers. The more numbers you guess, the bigger the winnings will be.

The second way: "Guess the parity"
There will be 20 numbers in the draw. Guess which numbers in the draw will be more - even or odd. If you choose even or odd and guess right, you win 170 ₽. If you choose "equally" and guess right - 340 ₽.

Third method: "Column selection"
There are 10 columns in the playing field of the Keno ticket. Choose which column will have more numbers in the draw than the others. If you guess right, you win 700 ₽.
